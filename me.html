About Me

Hi, I'm Alice! Welcome to my little corner of the web, where I share my passions for neuroscience, AI, and life adventure.

I’m currently working at Prof. John Detre’s lab at Penn Medicine, assisting with data analysis for the RECOVER program at Prof. David Fischer’s lab. I’ve always been curious about human consciousness, aging, philosophy, and brain-computer interfaces. Growing up watching sci-fi sparked my interest, leading me to major in Neuroscience at Colorado College (B.A.) and Biomedical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University (M.S.).

As I explored neuroscience, I became fascinated by AI—its efficiency in analyzing great volumes of data, finding patterns from the noise, and predicting outcomes with a network structure like our human brain. My dream is to become a scientist applying AI tools in biomedicine, contributing to research that helps patients with cognitive deficits and neurological disorders.

Outside of work, I enjoy snowboarding, surfing, traveling, and seeking the beauty and challenges of nature through sports!
